How Sound Swipe works

Why this app exists

English spelling is weird. Sometimes words do not sound the way they look.

Even if you know the correct English sounds, it can still be hard to know where to use them. The letter C can sound like /k/ in cat, but /s/ in city. The letters CH can sound like /tʃ/ in chair, /k/ in school, or /ʃ/ in machine.

These quizzes train your spelling-to-sound awareness. You are not just memorizing rules. You are building the instinct to look at a word and feel which sound belongs there.

With enough practice, you will start to recognize the sound by the pattern, the position, and the vibe of the word, even when the rule is not obvious.

IPA symbols

This app uses IPA symbols. IPA is a writing system for sounds.

Always tap the speaker buttons when you enter a lesson. Some symbols may look familiar, but mean something different.

For example, /j/ does not mean the J sound in joke. In IPA, /j/ is the Y sound in yes.

So when in doubt: tap, listen, then answer.
